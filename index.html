Undergraduate Admission Guide

Complete pathway to earning your Bachelor's degree at a US university

üí° Key Insight: US undergraduate admissions are holistic, considering academics, extracurriculars, essays, and personal qualities.
üìö

Academic Requirements

  • High School Transcripts: Official records with grade conversion
  • SAT/ACT: Most schools test-optional, but competitive scores help
  • English Proficiency: TOEFL (80+) or IELTS (6.5+)
  • GPA: Typically 3.0+ on 4.0 scale

Application Components

  • Personal Essay: Common App or university-specific
  • Recommendations: 2-3 letters from teachers/counselors
  • Activities List: Extracurriculars, leadership, volunteering
  • Portfolio: For arts/architecture programs

Graduate (Master's) Admission Guide

Advance your career with a Master's degree from a US university

üéØ

Requirements

  • Bachelor's Degree: From accredited institution
  • GRE/GMAT: GRE for most programs, GMAT for business
  • TOEFL/IELTS: 90+/7.0+ typically required
  • GPA: 3.0+ preferred, 3.5+ for top programs
  • Work Experience: Preferred for some programs (MBA, etc.)
üìù

Application Materials

  • Statement of Purpose: 500-1,000 words on goals
  • Letters of Recommendation: 2-3 from professors/employers
  • Resume/CV: Academic and professional experience
  • Writing Sample: For humanities/social sciences
  • Transcripts: Official from all universities attended

üí° Pro Tips for Master's Applications

‚úÖ DO: Tailor your statement to each program's strengths and your research interests
‚úÖ DO: Contact potential advisors before applying
‚ùå DON'T: Submit generic applications without program-specific details
‚ùå DON'T: Miss funding application deadlines (often earlier than admission deadlines)

Doctoral (PhD) Admission Guide

Pursue advanced research and become an expert in your field

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: PhD admissions are highly competitive and research-focused. Most US PhDs are fully funded with stipends.
üî¨

PhD Requirements

  • Master's Degree: Preferred but not always required
  • Research Experience: Critical - publications, projects
  • GRE: Required for many programs (some waiving)
  • Strong Academic Record: 3.5+ GPA typically
  • Faculty Match: Aligned research interests
üìÑ

Critical Documents

  • Research Statement: Detailed research proposal (2-3 pages)
  • Strong Recommendations: 3 from research supervisors
  • CV: Emphasize publications, presentations, grants
  • Writing Sample: Published paper or thesis chapter
  • Contact with Faculty: Email potential advisors early

üéØ Finding the Right PhD Program

  1. Research Faculty: Identify 2-3 potential advisors whose work aligns with yours
  2. Email Professors: Send concise emails expressing interest (September-November)
  3. Check Funding: Confirm program offers full funding + stipend
  4. Program Fit: Consider departmental culture, resources, and alumni outcomes
  5. Apply Strategically: 6-8 programs with varied competitiveness

Postdoctoral Position Guide

Advance your research career with a postdoctoral appointment

üß™ Understanding Postdoc Positions

Postdoctoral positions are temporary research appointments designed to provide additional training and experience beyond the PhD. They typically last 1-3 years.

üìã

Application Process

  • Direct Contact: Email potential PIs directly
  • Job Boards: Higher Ed Jobs, Nature Careers,Indeed
  • CV: Emphasize publications and research impact
  • Cover Letter: Research fit and future plans
  • References: 3 letters, including PhD advisor
üíº

Visa Options

  • J-1 Visa: Most common for postdocs
  • H-1B Visa: Available but less common
  • F-1 OPT: If transitioning from PhD
  • Salary Range: $50,000-$70,000 typically

Application Documents Guide

Prepare compelling application materials

üìù Statement of Purpose (SOP)

‚ñº

Structure:

  1. Introduction: Hook + background (1 paragraph)
  2. Academic Journey: Relevant experiences, research (2-3 paragraphs)
  3. Research Interests: Specific topics, why this program (2 paragraphs)
  4. Future Goals: Career aspirations, how degree helps (1 paragraph)
  5. Conclusion: Why you're a good fit (1 paragraph)
Length: 500-1,000 words (follow program guidelines)

‚úâÔ∏è Letters of Recommendation

‚ñº

Who to Ask:

  • Research supervisors who know your work deeply
  • Professors from relevant coursework (A grades)
  • Employers (for professional programs)

How to Ask:

  1. Ask 2-3 months before deadline
  2. Provide CV, statement, and program details
  3. Send reminder 2 weeks before deadline
  4. Waive right to view (shows confidence)

üìÑ Resume/CV

‚ñº

Academic CV Sections:

  • Education: Degrees, GPA, honors
  • Research Experience: Projects, methodologies, outcomes
  • Publications: Papers, presentations, posters
  • Teaching: TA positions, tutoring
  • Awards: Scholarships, fellowships, grants
  • Skills: Technical, language, software

üìú Transcripts

‚ñº
  • Official Transcripts: Sealed envelope from university
  • Grade Conversion: May need WES/ECE evaluation
  • Translation: Must be certified if not in English
  • All Institutions: Include every university attended

  • Fellowships: Competitive, full funding
  • Research Grants: Faculty-funded projects
  • External Funding: Fulbright, home country scholarships
PhD: Most US PhD programs offer full funding (5-6 years)

Student Visa Guide

Navigate the US student visa process

üõÇ

F-1 Student Visa

Process:

  1. Receive I-20 from university
  2. Pay SEVIS fee ($350)
  3. Complete DS-160 form
  4. Schedule visa interview
  5. Attend interview with documents

Required Documents:

  • Valid passport
  • I-20 form
  • Admission letter
  • Financial proof
  • SEVIS receipt
üîÑ

J-1 Exchange Visa

Common for:

  • Postdoctoral researchers
  • Visiting scholars
  • Some graduate programs

Key Difference:

J-1 visa holders receive DS-2019 instead of I-20. May have 2-year home residency requirement.

üíº Work Authorization

On-Campus Work

  • Up to 20 hours/week during semester
  • Full-time during breaks
  • No special authorization needed

CPT (Curricular Practical Training)

  • Internships related to studies
  • Before graduation
  • Must be part of curriculum

OPT (Optional Practical Training)

  • 12 months post-graduation
  • 24-month STEM extension available
  • Must be in field of study
